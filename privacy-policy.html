Privacy Policy

Application: Aloha Beer Internal Dashboard
Effective date: January 1, 2026
Last updated: January 1, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Aloha Beer Company, LP (“we,” “us,” or the “Company”) handles data within the Aloha Beer Internal Dashboard (the “Application”), an internal tool used by the Company to view and analyze its own business data. The Application is intended solely for use by the Company’s authorized personnel and is not offered to the public.

1. Information we access

The Application connects to the Company’s own QuickBooks Online account through Intuit’s official API, using OAuth 2.0 authorization granted by the Company. It accesses accounting and financial information on a read-only basis, including profit-and-loss and balance-sheet figures, chart of accounts, classes/departments, and related reporting data. The Application does not access QuickBooks data belonging to any party other than the Company, and it does not collect personal information from external end users.

2. How we use the information

Accessed data is used exclusively to generate internal financial and operational reports and dashboards for the Company’s own management and decision-making. We do not use the data for advertising, profiling, or any purpose unrelated to the Company’s internal reporting.

3. How the information is stored and secured

Data retrieved from QuickBooks Online is stored in a database controlled by the Company and is accessible only to authorized Company personnel through authenticated accounts. OAuth access and refresh tokens are stored securely and used only to maintain the Company’s own connection. We apply reasonable administrative and technical safeguards to protect the data against unauthorized access.

4. Information sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share the accessed data with any third party. The data is processed only within the Company’s own systems. We may disclose information if required by law.

5. Data retention

Data is retained for as long as it is needed for the Company’s internal reporting and is deleted or overwritten when no longer required. The Company may revoke the Application’s access to QuickBooks Online at any time from within its Intuit account, which stops further data access.

6. Intuit / QuickBooks

Use of QuickBooks Online data is subject to Intuit’s applicable terms. The Application accesses such data only with the Company’s authorization and only to the extent necessary to provide internal reporting.

7. Children’s privacy

The Application is an internal business tool and is not directed to children and does not knowingly collect information from anyone under 16.

8. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by updating the “Last updated” date above.
